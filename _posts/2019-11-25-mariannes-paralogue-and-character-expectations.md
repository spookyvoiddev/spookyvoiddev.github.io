While most missions in _Fire Emblem: Three Houses_ begin with Byleth (you) as the main character of the story, Marianne’s Paralogue begins a little differently. While walking through the monastery, you find a peasant harassing your student, Marianne, a student that’s known to be quite delicate and soft. She quickly calls you over and asks for help. As the righteous hero, you scare the peasant away. However, before doing so, he drops a bombshell: Marianne is accused of being a literal monster that drags away and feasts on villagers within the territory.

Marianne then reveals to you that her Crest (think inherent power given via bloodlines) is cursed, and is known as “The Crest of the Beast.” Marianne believes her Crest to be a symbol of disaster and misfortune. Suddenly, all the shyness and delicacy of this character is explained: she doesn’t want to cause trouble for people and therefore shuts herself in. She states that she doesn’t have the strength to defeat the monster on her own (and she doesn’t, she is one of the weakest units in the game!) and asks you and your class to come to assist her. If you agree, you have just signed yourself up for arguably the hardest mission in the game. At least, it was for me.

You are there at night because the beast only appears at night. You don’t know exactly what you’re fighting, but you’ve fought Black Beasts before. Generally, each one has about three health bars and various attacks that can hit multiple units; it’s not like you can simply on shot this thing and move on with your day. To make things worse, the map is covered in fog of war and tall brush. Meaning, you can’t see any unit 5 tiles away from your own, and the is defensive terrain for all units except flying units. So far, the odds are stacked against you. The Wandering Beast (hereby referred to as “Maurice”) has four health bars and is one of the strongest Black Beasts in the game. Oh, and to make the difficulty even harder, Marianne spawns right next to Maurice, on the opposite corner of the map from where the rest of your units spawn.

![](/assets/img/posts/tumblr_30b2a86d378dc0409be86296752484a6_8e214ae8_540-159ef1.jpg)

So, now not only do you have to make sure Marianne can evade all of Maurice’s attacks, but you also have to get her back to the rest of your party, all while avoiding the swarm of Black Beasts on the map. In a pre-combat cutscene, Maurice states, “This forest is the den of Demonic Beasts. You will be lucky to make it out alive.” And he doesn’t lie. This level is hard. The Demonic Beasts are immune to various kinds of attacks, so some units will simply be useless. Other times, Marianne will die and the mission will restart. Or, if you are playing on “Classic” difficulty, a game mode that enables permadeath, you might be like me and restart every time any unit dies.

The fog of war allows the developers to continue spawning Demonic Beats in spots the player’s units can’t see, as well as allowing Maurice to vanish on one end of the map and appear next to your weaker units on the other.

The fear that the peasant expressed in the intro cutscene is real, Maurice and the Demonic Beasts are terrifying. What I see happen in most playthroughs (and in my own) is the player being forced to adopt a “square” formation, where the important/ranged units are in the center of a square formed by tanky/fighter units. This allows the player to systematically clear the map one by one, and because all the units are huddled, the fear of someone being caught in the open is negated.

What I realized is that this formation is Marianne’s entire life. Her entire life, she is shut in and she puts others around her like a wall. She deflects, is shy, and prefers the company of animals to that of humans. The fact that her Paralogue, the mission entirely crafted for her, begins with her confronting the strongest Black Beast in the game is a testament to her willpower. She doesn’t want your help but needs it.

When Maurice is defeated, he thanks his descendant and disappears, leaving behind an heirloom for Marianne, a sword based on their Crest. While the sword is powerful on its own, Marianne’s Crest increases its base bonuses, enough to consider turning her into a combat unit. This character development, a unit going from a medical/support unit to a combat unit late into the game is unique, and one that I hadn’t seen in any tactics game before. Generally, they discourage not beelining for a certain class or bonus, but in Marianne’s case, it’s almost as though the developers are winking at the players.

Marianne expresses her joy to the protagonist, and she states, “Without your help, I would never have found peace.” Now, to sour the mood a little, this Paralogue happens post-time skip in Fire Emblem. Depending on the decisions made prior, the player may have not recruited Marianne. If she is not recruited, Marianne disappears from the game entirely. There is no mention of her in the post-credits (where all the characters can be seen living their lives after the game,) she isn’t fought in combat, and she is never mentioned. This simply doesn’t happen with the other characters, they each are at least mentioned/fought/seen/etc.

The only reason I can see for this is that Marianne commits suicide if the player does not recruit her. She has found herself in constant internal conflict, and the line the speaks to the player is intensely profound. Constantly throughout the game she has been physically weak and frail, constantly having to be defended by your other units. However, she soon becomes an independent and powerful unit, and much like her character in the cutscenes, she begins to rely less and less on other units in your party, and she soon becomes a combatant in her own right. This made her less of a “unit” for me and more of a character. Seeing that growth was important for me, and it isn’t something that I see a lot of in video games, much less a “tactics” based one.

Marianne’s Paralogue is wildly important **to Marianne**. The conditions are simple: to find peace, Marianne must put a stop to Maurice. That is the only way for her to begin the road to recovery. That is the obstacle in her life. You, as the player, have the power to deny her that. You can simply refuse her request, avoid the most difficult mission in the game, and move on like normal. However, you miss out on amazing character growth and development, and true happiness in a character that resonated with me on a personal level.
