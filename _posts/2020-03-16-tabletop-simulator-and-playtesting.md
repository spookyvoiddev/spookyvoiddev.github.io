With COVID-19, many of us have found ourselves working remotely and from the comfort of our homes. For game developers, this can be challenging as working with another human is one of the main parts of development! It especially is so for me, I love human interaction, and I found myself lacking that during this self-quarantine we are all undergoing.

Additionally, I find myself in the peculiar position of working on a tabletop game at the same time. Originally meant for my Game Systems class, Peasant Warfare has become a personal project for our group, and we have decided to continue its development. Working remotely on a tabletop game is DIFFICULT, unlike digital games, we have no way of playtesting without others. Until Tabletop Simulator.

While a bit rough around the edges, TTS allows users to upload graphics and assets up directly into the Steam Cloud and into the workshop, allowing players to quickly prototype new games. I did this. I uploaded our glorious artwork onto the workshop and quickly began ~~spamming~~ asking users in the global chat to playtest our game. I got 2 lucky (or unlucky) individuals, and we immediately began.

They took to our game fairly quickly, and using the notebook feature, I was able to include a reference for them at all times. I gotta say, this playtest was much smoother than any of our in person playtests. The feedback non-students gave us was eye-opening, and I soon found myself thinking about things that I hadn’t even considered before.
