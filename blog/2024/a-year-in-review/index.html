A Year In Review

Created on January 11, 2024

2024   ·   Gaming   personal   writing

Well, it’s been quite a while since I’ve posted here! Thank you to my one monthly reader. I see you. I suppose it’s only fair that I update you. You’ve been so faithful, after all!

Back in April 2022, I made a post discussing my (at the time) recent Roll20 marketplace aspirations. I am happy to say that it was a very financially successful endeavor, and I have made back the money in assets and then some. Ironstone Press, while on the backburner now, is not something I thought would work out the way it did. I am surprised and baffled by the audience’s response to my maps. It was truly heartwarming to see many people buy and use my maps! As a developer, this is the first time that I’ve gotten to experience others purchasing and playing my work. It was magical.

Throughout the process, I had been searching for jobs in the industry. Thankfully, and luckily, the search did not take too long! In just a few short months, I managed to find a QA position at a company that profoundly impacted my childhood. What are the odds?!

The Behemoth!

The Behemoth swooped in (ha!) and took my life for a change! No longer employed at Burlington, I quickly began my first job in the games industry. At Behemoth, I got to work on a few games that I am really happy with the production value and quality of. Alien Hominid Invasion, our newest title (Released on most platforms last November,) and the remaster of Alien Hominid HD were both incredible experiences to learn and ship.

My time at Behemoth has been filled with joy, happiness, and thrill. I was very happy to come to work most days, sit down, and write some bugs. Wait, my time? Is this going to get sad?!

Unfortunately, I am but a contract worker in a full-time world. While they have opted to extend my contract for the time being, I do not imagine this will be a permanent situation. Eventually, my time at Behemoth will come to an end. For now? I’m quite happy where I am, and I am thankful for the opportunity I had.

Thank you for reading, my one reader, I appreciate you! Youcanclickl3 to me is my little pocket of the world. Despite all the pain, suffering, and general discomfort the outside world can cause, my little blog will be my sunshine.

P.S., I just watched Willy Wonka and the Chocolate Factory for the first time. Absolutely amazing! I think I’m in love. I wish I had watched this film as a kid to truly experience the magic. Oh well, better late than never!

Thanks for reading, reader. See you next time.
